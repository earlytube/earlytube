✦ Our story

Built in one night.
For every parent
who isn't too late.

EarlyTube started with a simple, heartbreaking observation — and a question that wouldn't let go.

My child is on the autism spectrum. Like many kids, they discovered YouTube — and with it, the rewind button. What followed was a pattern I couldn't unsee: the same one-second clip, over and over and over. Not watching a video. Stimming on it.

The behaviour had a name. It had a cause. And by the time I understood what was happening, the habit was already deeply set. My child was too smart, too used to it. There was no going back.

"But what about the child who was diagnosed yesterday? What if they were never introduced to the rewind button in the first place? What if they never know?"

That question sat with me for a while. Then one night, I decided to stop waiting for someone else to build it. I opened my laptop and didn't stop until EarlyTube existed.

It's not a cure. It's not therapy. It's a small, free tool — a version of YouTube that simply removes the controls. No rewind. No skip. No progress bar to scrub. Just the video, playing forward, as it was meant to be watched.

For parents of newly diagnosed children, this could matter enormously. The habit that took hold in my child might never form in yours — if you start here, before they know there's anything else.

No controls. Ever.
No rewind, no skip, no progress bar. An invisible layer blocks all interaction with the player. The video just plays.
📌
Parent PIN to exit
Closing a video requires a 4-digit PIN. The child stays in a safe, controlled viewing experience until a parent decides otherwise.
📋
Curated educational videos
Age-appropriate suggestions from trusted channels. Parents can also paste any YouTube link directly — they stay the gatekeeper of what gets watched.
